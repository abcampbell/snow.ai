What this is NOT: a hedge for SONIA. Oil long and SONIA long correlate -0.19 — modest natural offset. But adding a binary catalyst on top of a leveraged binary catalyst is two coin flips, not a balanced book. Size accordingly.
What I would NOT do at OVX 75.8%:

  • Roll into deeper OTM grain calls as a "supply shock" hedge — your ag book already has $33k+ of theta-bleeding optionality there. Different supply chain, different catalyst, no reason to double up.
How the pricer works

Pull CL=F spot and ^OVX from yfinance.
OVX is the CBOE Oil ETF Volatility Index — implied vol on USO ATM options, ~ proxy for WTI ATM IV.
Apply OTM skew: ATM IV + 5pp per 20% OTM, capped at 1.5× ATM. (Crude options exhibit positive call skew during supply-shock fear regimes.)
Run Black-76 against each strike: C = e^(-rT) · [F·N(d1) - K·N(d2)].
Spread debit = lo-strike call mid − hi-strike call mid. Max gain = (K_hi − K_lo) − debit. Multiplier = 100 (MCL micro contract).
All %NLV figures use $112,251 reference NLV; replace as needed.

Caveats — why this isn't a quote

Black-76 fair value, not market mid. Real bid/ask at IB will likely have 5-10% spread on these strikes. NYMEX market data subscription would give exact quotes; we don't have one on this machine, hence the model approach.
OVX is USO-derived, not direct CL=F IV. Front-month WTI vol has historically been ~5-10pp above OVX. So if anything, this pricer slightly UNDERESTIMATES premium — real spreads cost more than shown.
OTM skew approximation is generic; actual NYMEX vol surface in fear regimes can be steeper. Treat the % NLV numbers as ±20% accuracy.
