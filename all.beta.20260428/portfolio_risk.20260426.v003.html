The actual book character (corrected): You're running a 13.8% ann vol book — moderate, not aggressive. The risk concentration is in the options book (sugar + grains + tech) not the SONIA leftover. SONIA's nominal delta is huge (+223% NLV) but its vol contribution is tiny (~2% of variance) because 3M STIR futures barely move in price terms.

So "is SONIA too big" reframed: No, in vol terms it's a small position. Yes, in "what could go wrong if I'm completely wrong on UK rates" terms (binary catalyst risk) it's a meaningful negative-carry trade. The wrong question was "how do I balance SONIA?" — the right question is "how much P&L bleed do I tolerate while waiting for peace?"
Real concentration: ag options + gold options. Sugar (25.5% of risk) + Gold (26.7%) = 52% of variance. Then small caps (8%), corn (7%), power (6%). These are the real positions to look at, not SONIA.

What this means for the Hormuz crude trade: adding 2× Dec broken fly + 1× Aug at ~0.6% NLV cost is reasonable — total Crude becomes ~5% of risk (vs 4% today). Doesn't shift the book character. Don't over-size — at 5× crude flies, crude becomes the biggest single risk and you've replaced "ag-options book with a SONIA tail" with "ag-options book + crude-options book with a SONIA tail."
Known accuracy gaps (do not over-interpret decimals):
  • SHY proxy for SONIA — 1.7% vol is closer than TLT's 14% but still not SONIA-exact. Real SONIA vol is ~0.6-1.2% based on rate-curve moves YTD. SHY overstates by ~30-40%.
  • Delta-normal VaR ignores gamma/vega. 0.87% daily 1σ doesn't include vol shocks. A real ag dislocation could move the book +/-5% NLV in a day not visible here, only in the named scenarios.
  • Sugar option strikes with marketPrice=0 default to 30% IV → δ=0. ~4 of 22 sugar positions affected (small).
  • 1-year covariance lookback. Captures recent regime, not stress.
