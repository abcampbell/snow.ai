# Research Anchors

## Core references

- Bridgewater, "The All Weather Story"
  https://www.bridgewater.com/research-and-insights/the-all-weather-story

- Meir Statman (1987), "How Many Stocks Make a Diversified Portfolio?"
  https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-financial-and-quantitative-analysis/article/how-many-stocks-make-a-diversified-portfolio/CE5CDF2C7225FC1E0EDE3E700A3C66A7

- AQR, "Understanding Risk Parity"
  https://www.aqr.com/-/media/AQR/Documents/Insights/White-Papers/Understanding-Risk-Parity.pdf

- Attilio Meucci, "Re-Defining and Managing Diversification"
  https://www.bayes.city.ac.uk/__data/assets/pdf_file/0003/213699/Meucci-ReDefining-and-Managing-Diversification.pdf

- James, Menzies, Gottwald (2022), "On financial market correlation structures and diversification benefits across and within equity sectors"
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.10623

## Why these matter

- Statman gives the classic 30-40 stock benchmark for diversifying a random stock portfolio.
- Bridgewater gives the cleanest primary-source framing for separating beta from alpha and balancing environmental exposures.
- AQR helps explain why risk parity is about reallocating risk away from equity concentration, not just changing capital weights.
- Meucci is useful for the "effective bets" / concentration-vs-diversification language.
- James et al. gives a recent empirical reminder that equity diversification gets weaker when collective behavior and correlations rise.
